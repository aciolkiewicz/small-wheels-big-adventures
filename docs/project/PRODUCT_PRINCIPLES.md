# Product Principles

## 1. Family First

Every decision starts with one question:

Does this help families spend more quality time together?

If not, it probably does not belong in the product.

---

## 2. Content Before Features

Content is the product.

The website, mobile application and PDF are only different ways of delivering it.

Whenever possible:

Create once.

Publish everywhere.

---

## 3. Trust Over Growth

We never recommend places we would not personally recommend.

Editorial trust is more important than publishing new content quickly.

---

## 4. Reduce Planning Time

The goal is not to show attractions.

The goal is to reduce planning time.

A successful route should answer all practical questions before the family leaves home.

---

## 5. Experience Over Distance

The best trip is not the longest one.

It is the one that creates the best family memories.

---

## 6. Practical Information Wins

Every article should answer practical questions.

Where to park?

Where to eat?

Where is the toilet?

Where can the child play?

How much shade is available?

Can a bike trailer pass?

---

## 7. Simplicity Scales

Complexity is added only when absolutely necessary.

Simple products are easier to maintain and easier to understand.

---

## 8. Reusable Content

Every piece of content should be reusable.

One description should work in:

- website
- mobile app
- PDF
- newsletter

---

## 9. Long-term Thinking

We optimize for the next five years, not the next five weeks.

---

## 10. Editorial Independence

Commercial opportunities must never influence recommendations.
